Artwork

Needs More Llama

This "Bougereau Remastered" piece takes an original painting by the 19th-century French painter, William-Adolphe Bouguereau, and replaces the sheep with a llama. The major challenge I faced with this composite was making the llama photos match the lighting and brush strokes of the original work. The llama is a composite of two different llama photos because I wanted that face but needed a full body.

Springtime on Mars

Springtime on Mars is a composite piece made of Mars surface and deep space photos sourced from NASA mixed with images of everyday Earth clouds. I wanted this piece to feel both alien and familar; extrodinary and pedestrian.

At first glance this scene could easily be mistaken for a desert on Earth but with a little insight granted by the title it takes on a whole new meaning.

Michigan Summers

This piece is a reflection on some of my favorite times during my Michigan years. It is on the shores of Lake Michigan in St. Joseph, Michigan. Michigan is mostly known for bleak snow but during the few warm months the beaches come alive. This composite is a sunset on the 4th of July. Recreational boats gathered here, everyone anxiously awaiting the sunset before the fireworks show. The slices were taken at different times painting the sky with a variety of shades and hues.

Cycle

This self-portrait is a piece of visualized introspection at a time when I had to re-evaluate everything I held true. It depicts the ambivalence of stagnation and progress; trying to reconcile the past while facing an uncertain future.
